# Brand Voice - Dengo

## Brand Personality

### Core Personality Traits
**Acolhedor (Welcoming)** - Warm, inviting, and approachable
- Creates a safe space for emotional exploration
- Makes users feel understood and accepted
- Emphasizes comfort and emotional security

**Leve (Light)** - Gentle, easy, and non-pressured
- Doesn't feel like work or therapy
- Emphasizes the joy and playfulness of connection
- Reduces anxiety around relationship topics

**Inspirador (Inspirational)** - Motivating and hopeful
- Encourages growth and positive change
- Highlights the potential in relationships
- Focuses on possibilities rather than problems

### Tone Characteristics
- **Conversational** - Natural, friendly, like talking to a trusted friend
- **Empathetic** - Understanding and validating of relationship challenges
- **Positive** - Focused on growth, connection, and possibilities
- **Respectful** - Honoring the intimacy and privacy of relationships
- **Gentle** - Soft approach to potentially sensitive topics

## Voice Guidelines

### What We Do
- **Use inclusive language** that welcomes all types of couples
- **Acknowledge relationship challenges** with empathy and understanding
- **Celebrate small wins** and daily progress in relationships
- **Provide gentle guidance** without being prescriptive
- **Focus on connection** rather than fixing problems
- **Maintain warmth** and emotional accessibility
- **Encourage curiosity** and discovery about each other

### What We Don't Do
- **Use clinical language** that feels cold or therapeutic
- **Make assumptions** about relationship dynamics
- **Apply pressure** or create anxiety about relationship performance
- **Judge or criticize** relationship choices or stages
- **Focus on problems** without highlighting solutions
- **Use gendered assumptions** about roles in relationships
- **Be overly explicit** about intimate topics

## Language Examples

### Correct vs. Incorrect Language

**CORRECT:**
- "Descubra algo novo sobre seu parceiro hoje"
- "Pequenos passos para conexão profunda"
- "Converse sobre o que importa para vocês dois"
- "Transforme seu dia a dia em momentos de conexão"
- "Explore sua intimidade emocional com gentileza"

**INCORRECT:**
- "Resolva os problemas do seu relacionamento"
- "Teste o quão bem você conhece seu parceiro"
- "Diagnostic your relationship issues"
- "Forced intimacy exercises"
- "Relationship therapy substitute"

### Writing Style Examples

**Conversational Approach:**
- "Hoje que tal explorar algo novo sobre seu parceiro?"
- "Que tal uma pergunta que aproxima?"
- "Vamos criar um ritual diário de conexão?"

**Instead of:**
- "Execute relationship strengthening exercise"
- "Complete daily connection assessment"
- "Initiate intimacy protocol"

## Brand Expression Across Touchpoints

### In-App Messaging
- **Welcome messages:** "Que bom que vocês estão aqui! Vamos começar a fortalecer sua conexão."
- **Daily prompts:** "Hoje temos uma pergunta especial para vocês dois."
- **Progress messages:** "Parabéns por manter o ritual de conexão!"
- **Encouragement:** "Cada conversa importa, por menor que seja."

### Marketing Copy
- **Taglines:** "Conexão diária para casais modernos"
- **Descriptions:** "Transforme o cuidado com sua relação em um hábito leve e prazeroso"
- **Social media:** "Pequenas perguntas, grandes conexões"
- **Emails:** "Sua dose diária de conexão está pronta"

### Customer Support
- **Tone:** Understanding and patient
- **Language:** Clear and supportive
- **Approach:** Solution-focused and empathetic
- **Response style:** Personal and caring

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Portuguese (Brazilian) Considerations
- Use formal and informal language appropriately
- Respect diverse relationship structures
- Acknowledge Brazilian relationship cultural norms
- Avoid regional stereotypes or assumptions
- Consider family dynamics in Brazilian culture

### English (US) Considerations
- Use inclusive language for diverse couple types
- Avoid gendered assumptions
- Respect different cultural backgrounds
- Acknowledge various relationship stages and dynamics
- Consider work-life balance challenges

## Emotional Resonance

### Feelings We Want to Evoke
- **Safety:** Users feel secure exploring intimacy
- **Hope:** Optimistic about relationship growth
- **Connection:** Feeling closer to their partner
- **Joy:** Enjoyment in the daily practice
- **Understanding:** Better insight into their relationship

### Emotional Guardrails
- **Avoid anxiety:** Don't create pressure or worry
- **Prevent inadequacy:** Don't make users feel like they're failing
- **Respect boundaries:** Honor individual comfort levels
- **Maintain privacy:** Protect intimate sharing
- **Preserve autonomy:** Support self-directed exploration

## Consistency Markers

### Voice Check Questions
Before any communication, ask:
1. Does this sound like a caring friend rather than a therapist?
2. Would this make someone feel welcomed and understood?
3. Does this respect the intimacy and privacy of relationships?
4. Is this approach gentle and non-pressured?
5. Does this celebrate connection rather than highlight problems?

### Brand Voice Audit
- Review all content quarterly for voice consistency
- Gather user feedback on brand perception
- Train all team members on voice guidelines
- Create voice reference materials for new content creators
- Monitor social media for voice consistency
